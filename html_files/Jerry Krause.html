Jerry Krause Passes Away



Canada: Along with the six titles, Krause was honored with the league's NBA Executive of the Year Award twice.Jerry Krause, the general manager as the Chicago Bulls franchise that won six NBA championships in eight seasons, died on Tuesday after battling persistent health issues, including osteomyelitis. A finalist for the Naismith Basketball Hall of Fame as a contributor, Krause put the key pieces together around Michael Jordan, who was drafted by his predecessor Rod Thorn, which later established the Bulls as an NBA dynasty. Nicknamed "The Sleuth" because of his secretive ways and scouting acumen, Krause drafted Hall of Famer Scottie Pippen and Horace Grant in the 1987 NBA draft and traded Charles Oakley, despite Jordan's protestations, to the Knicks for starting center Bill Cartwright. He worked as a scout for several NBA organizations before joining the Bulls and returned to scouting baseball prospects following his departure from the NBA. He also drafted Toni Kukoc in the second round of the NBA Draft and traded for future Hall of Famer Dennis Rodman.
