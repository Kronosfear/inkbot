Australia: As the story detonated on Monday — as only Collingwood stories can — numerous observers recalled that De Goey, when he first arrived at the club, had pledged to Buckley “I won’t let you down.” De Goey, doubtless, feels terrible about his betrayal of the coach’s trust and will want to redeem himself as quickly as possible. While De Goey is not Adam Treloar or Scott Pendlebury, Buckley has accumulated more pre-season injuries/suspension than is ideal for a coach whose career (as he admitted last year) probably hangs on whether his team makes the eight. IT IS quite possible that this latest pre-season calamity at Collingwood will be the making of Jordan de Goey, whom Collingwood harboured concerns about last year. The transformation of Jordan De Goey, if it takes place, could well benefit Buckley’s successor, rather than the coach who chose to believe, both in the player and his work of fiction. Buckley is entitled to be livid with De Goey and to wonder whether he is cursed as coach.
