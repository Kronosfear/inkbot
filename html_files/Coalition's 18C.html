Coalition agrees to push 18C changes, despite warning from Joyce



Australia: Coalition agrees to push 18C changes, despite warning from Joyce Prime Minister Malcolm Turnbull addressed the party room at Parliament House in Canberra on Tuesday 29 November 2016 Photo: Andrew Meares Photo: Andrew Meares Tweet Facebook of ╳ exit Australia's race hate laws have lost credibility, Prime Minister Malcolm Turnbull says, and fixing them requires the removal of legal prohibitions on people's right to "offend, insult or humiliate", while making it illegal to "harass". Former prime minister Tony Abbott praised Mr Turnbull and Senator Brandis during the party room discussion, while Development Minister Concetta Fierravanti-Wells warned changing section 18C of the Racial Discrimination Act would be very unpopular with multicultural communities. Topics: discrimination, government-and-politics, sydney-2000 First postedDragging out debate about Australia's race hate laws will cost the Coalition votes, Deputy Prime Minister Barnaby Joyce has warned during an hour long debate in the Coalition party room. Mr Joyce's warning to the party room was in line with similar comments he has made about the fractious same-sex marriage debate - that, essentially, voters are not concerned about section 18C but are more concerned about day to day issues and that the Coalition should stop talking about the issue. In a move that has delighted the right of the Coalition and dismayed MPs who hold marginal seats or represent ethnic communities, Mr Turnbull and Attorney-General George Brandis unveiled changes to the language of section 18C of the Racial Discrimination Act.
