Miami Dolphins give tender to Kiko Alonso



USA: This tender can theoretically net the Dolphins a first round pick should Alonso receive a better offer from another team that Miami didn’t want to match, but that is not an option the team is considering, according to multiple reports, instead electing to build around the 26 year old. The team added former Steelers linebacker Lawrence Timmons to the fold, but Alonso's contract extension makes it seem like he's the answer in the middle. The Miami Dolphins are giving the middle linebacker a first-round tender offer worth $3.91 million. Salguero passed along further information about the situation: A source close to Alonso tells the Miami Herald that Alonso doesn't want to move from the middle linebacker spot in 2017. He spent his first season in Miami operating as an effective middle linebacker, but there's been talk about potentially moving him to the outside in the future.
